What is Plant Biology?

Plant Biology, also known as Botany, is a scientific branch of biology or life science that focuses on plants and plant life. It covers a wide range and depth of numerous subtopics that make up the subfield. Plant biology can be broadly classified into two categories at the highest level: first, as basic fundamental research, and second, as applied plant sciences that expand upon fundamental science findings and innovates to create new technologies that are then applied to agriculture. The fundamental four (4) main categories of plant biology are identified as follows: (1) plant anatomy, (2) plant morphology and evolution, (3) Physiology of plants; and (4) Molecular and cellular biology of plants. Similarly, Flora will cover these main categories of plant biology in this site: Plant Structure, Plant Function, and Plant Evolution.
