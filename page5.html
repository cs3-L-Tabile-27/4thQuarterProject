Plant Evolution

The evolution of plants can be traced back more than a hundred million years ago. It is believed that plants originated from an aquatic green alga protist that was probably related to the stonewort. Later on, they evolved with crucial land adaptations like seeds, flowers, and vascular tissues. Plants became far more successful and more adapted to life on dry land as a result of each of these significant changes. The order of plant evolution is arranged as follows: 1. The ancestral streptophyte algae's migration to land. 2. Vascular systems evolved in the earliest land plants. 3. Plants that produce seeds evolved. 4. Plants that bloomed evolved. Also, the four major phases of plant evolution on land began in the Ordovician period in the Palaeozoic era and lasted until the Cretaceous period in the Mesozoic era. Early land plant evolution took place over a period of about 345 million years.
